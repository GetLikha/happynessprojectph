About Us

Founded in 2019, the HappYness Project
Philippines (HPPH) is a movement of kindness
funded by family and friends. We are a SEC and
BIR registered and non-govermental organization.

HP creates year-round charitable activities
focused on education, socio-civic services
and community development.

HPPH creates year-round charitable activities
focused on education, socio-civic services and
community development.

Vision

Improve the quality of life in local
communities through holistic programs and
services.

Mission

To organize charitable projects related to education, cultural and socio-civic services,
community development, disaster relief,
scientific and technological advancement
and medical care.

Our Work

As we endeavor to make helping easy, we work together with individuals, friends, families, groups, and companies by organizing year-round charitable projects to uplift the condition and well-being of our local communities.

We have completed over 380 outreach activities and counting in over 65 communities around the Philippines - this is as of June end of month. What a feat!

During the pandemic, we raised close to 4M pesos to regularly provide personal protective equipment, meals, groceries, and other supplies to 52 hospitals, 40 bureaus of fire stations and police stations, 10 orphanages, and elderly shelters.

We are happiness spelled with a ‘Y’ because we believe that the You can change the world, so say Yes to life!

For the first half of the year 2023, we turned over donations to Duyan, the Red Ribbon project which is a shelter for children born with HIV/AIDS, the National Children’s Hospital cancer ward, House of Somang Home for the Elderly, Divisoria Community Pantry for Street Vendors, elderly home of the House of the Golden Gays, Aeta Community in Pampanga, and Ang Bahay Parola Children’s Home.

HP also serve our youth through seminars and workshops that will prepare their skills for future employment. We serve a total of forty-five 4th year college students in partnership with JP Morgan Chase & Co Corporate Centers Philippines Women on the Move Interactive Network- K12 Uplift.

These initiatives are fully funded through the HappY Hearts model.

In January 2023, we hired two social workers and launched our second segment called HappY Kids mentoring program in partnership with the local government of Barangay Molino Dos in Cavite.

We mentor 50 kids who were classified as part of the poorest of the poor in the survey of 44k inhabitants in the community.

Currently, 50 kids are what we can sustainably support. The dream is to graduate all of them to Senior High School.

As we were working and mentoring the kids, we realized that holistic change for our HappY Kids is only possible if we involve their parents, specifically their mothers.

In May 2023, we launched the HappY Nanays Livelihood Program where we introduced training for the HappY Nanays on rag making which we can sell in the market to augment their incomes.

Objective:

to raise healthy, confident, prayerful, God believing and equipped children to face life successfully

HappY Kids Project

Our goal is to teach our HappY Kids to read and comprehend, help them cope with schoolwork, practice song, dance, art and learn about social graces, good manners and personal hygiene, most of all, help them finish school and be meaningful and productive members of the society.

Currently we mentor and feed 54 children regularly. In December 2022, we were able to serve over 300 children in our various activities and programs.

HappY Nanays

The HappYness Project Philippines vision is to create a world where stay-at-home mothers have the resources, opportunities, and support necessary to build meaningful and sustainable livelihoods.

We envision a society that recognizes and values the skills, talents, and potential of stay-at-home mothers, enabling them to overcome barriers and achieve economic independence.

By providing comprehensive programs, training, and mentorship, we aim to empower these women to unlock their full potential, pursue fulfilling careers, and contribute to their families' and communities' well-being.

Through our collective efforts, we strive to create a future where stay-at-home mothers are empowered, financially secure, and play an active role in shaping a better tomorrow for themselves and their children.


HappY Nanays Livelihood Program

The HappYness Rag-Making Project shares a goal that includes: learn and develop skills, provide opportunity for micro business, women empowerment and help vulnerable families attain financial stability.
