About Us

Founded in 2019, the HappYness Project
Philippines (HPPH) is a movement of kindness
funded by family and friends. We are a SEC and
BIR registered and non-govermental organization.

HPPH creates year-round charitable activities
focused on education, socio-civic services and
community development.

Vision

Improve the quality of life in local communities
through holistic programs and services.

Mission

To organize charitable projects related to
education, cultural and socio-civic services,
community development, religion, disaster relief,
scientific and technological advancement and
medical care.

Our Story

HappYness Project is a movement of kindness

We are SEC-registered nonprofit organization that creates year-round activities focused on education, socio-civic service, and community development.

We are happiness spelled with a ‘Y’ because we believe that the You can change the world, so say Yes to life! We have completed over 350 outreach activities in over 65 communities around the Philippines since we started in 2018. What a feat!

During the pandemic, we raised close to 4M pesos to regularly provide personal protective equipment, meals, groceries and other supplies to 52 hospitals, 40 bureau of fire stations and police stations, 10 orphanages and elderly shelters.

There are three programs in HappYness Project. The HappY Hearts Partnerships where we connect individuals and corporations to our vetted partner institutions of orphanages, elderly homes, shelters, communities and schools. We will help you plan, organize and execute your funded outreach activities. We will do this service at no extra cost.

For the first half of this year, we are set to visit and turn over donations to Duyan, the red ribbon project which is a shelter for children born with HIV/AIDS, the National Children’s Hospital cancer ward, House of Somang Home for the Elderly, Divisoria Community Pantry for Street Vendors, and the elderly home of the House of the Golden Gays. These initiatives are fully funded through HappY Hearts model.

Not all stories
begin with once
upon a time.

In Jan 2023, we hired two social workers and launched our second segment called HappY Kids mentoring program in partnership with the local government of Barangay Molino Dos in Cavite. We mentor 50 kids who were classified as part of the poorest of the poor in the survey of 44k inhabitants in the community. Currently, 50 kids is what we can sustainably support.

In our mentoring facility, we teach the kids aged 4 to 15 how to read and comprehend, help them cope with schoolwork, practice song, dance, art and learn about social graces, good manners and personal hygiene.

More importantly, we feed them one rice meal per day. This is the only rice meal that the kids may have for the day as they do not regularly have access to food at home.

The dream is to graduate all of them to Senior high school. You see, by helping these 50, we are essentially changing the lives of 151 because our 50 kids have 101 other siblings who will benefit from their education.

The goal is to eventually multiply the HappY Kids program in other parts of the Philippines. We are actually working on our 2nd HappY kids mentoring center in Oriental Mindoro to be launched in August 2023 and will benefit the katutubos or indigenous mangyan community there.

As we were working and mentoring the kids, we realized that wholistic change for our HappY Kids is only possible if we involve their parents, specifically their mothers. In May 2023, we launched the HappY Nanays Livelihood Program where we have introduced training for the HappY Nanays on rag making which we can sell in the market to augment their incomes.

The next plan is to start our own urban garden so that the food that we feed our HappY Kids and HappY Nanays come from a sustainable source, helping cut our overhead costs. The savings will go into purchase of equipment to continue the trainings we provide to our HappY Nanays.

As we help them create an income stream, the living conditions of our HappY Kids will also be improved.
